# Ga-English Linguistic Annotation & AI Localization

This repository documents my research and practical work in **Linguistic Annotation**, focusing on the **Ga language** (Accra, Ghana). My goal is to improve the accuracy of Large Language Models (LLMs) in West African contexts.

## 🎯 Project Overview
- **Native Fluency:** Leveraging deep cultural and grammatical knowledge of Ga.
- **Sentiment Analysis:** Annotating Ga-English code-switching patterns in digital communication.
- **AI Safety:** Identifying "hallucinations" or logical errors in AI-generated Ga translations.

## 🛠️ Annotation Skills
- [x] **Semantic Labeling:** Identifying parts of speech and intent.
- [x] **RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback):** Ranking AI responses based on cultural relevance and linguistic accuracy.
- [x] **NLI (Natural Language Inference):** Checking if Ga translations logically follow their English prompts.

## 📚 Linguistic Nuances Tracked
* **Tone Markers:** Managing the high/low tones in Ga words that change meaning (e.g., *ba* - to come vs *bá* - to come/future).
* **Idiomatic Expressions:** Bridging the gap between literal and figurative meanings for AI training.

## 🚀 Current Academic Focus
As a **Computer Science student at GCTU**, I am bridging the gap between computational logic and native linguistics to build safer, more inclusive AI models.
